## 1. Target Customer Profile

### 1.1 Ideal Company Profile

| Attribute | Criteria | Rationale |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Company Size** | 500-5,000 employees in China | Large enough to have international teams, small enough to move fast |
| **Revenue** | ¥500M-¥5B annual revenue | Budget for training tools exists |
| **International Exposure** | ≥20% employees interact with global teams weekly | Clear pain point exists |
| **HQ Location** | China-based with foreign HQs (US/EU/India) | Cultural friction is real |
| **Tech Maturity** | Already uses Slack, Zoom, Microsoft Teams | Ready for digital training tools |

### 1.2 Industry Verticals (Prioritized)

| Priority | Industry | Examples | Win Probability |
|----------|----------|----------|-----------------|
| 1 | Tech (Product/Dev teams) | ByteDance, Alibaba International, Tencent Cloud | 70% |
| 2 | Consulting | McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte, EY China | 65% |
| 3 | Fintech/Banking | HSBC, Standard Chartered, Ant Group | 60% |
| 4 | Pharma/Medical | Roche, Novartis, AstraZeneca China | 55% |
| 5 | Manufacturing (Global) | Tesla China, Siemens, Bosch | 50% |

### 1.3 Buyer Personas

**Primary Decision Maker: VP of HR / L&D Director**
- Pain: High turnover of international-ready talent
- Goal: Reduce training costs while improving outcomes
- Budget authority: ¥50K-¥200K for pilot programs
- Timeline: Q1-Q3 for annual budget deployment

**Secondary Influencer: Engineering Manager / Team Lead**
- Pain: Daily communication friction with offshore teams
- Goal: Faster project delivery, fewer miscommunications
- Influence: Strong recommendation power
- Timeline: Immediate pain, advocates for solutions

**Economic Buyer: CFO / Finance Controller**
- Pain: Training spend with unclear ROI
- Goal: Measurable outcomes, cost per employee
- Budget authority: Final sign-off above ¥100K
- Timeline: End of quarter budget reviews

### 1.4 Pain Points (Quantified)

| Pain Point | Source | Impact |
|------------|--------|--------|
| Miscommunication causes project delays | 34% of global projects fail due to communication | ¥2M+ per delayed project |
| Talent retention | 28% leave due to "international readiness stress" | ¥500K+ replacement cost |
| Meeting inefficiency | 30% of meeting time is "what did you say?" | 3 hrs/week × ¥200/hr × 100 people = ¥3M/year |
| Training programs ineffective | Traditional language training doesn't address workplace context | ¥5K/employee wasted annually |

### 1.5 Decision-Making Process

```
Week 1-2:    Problem identification (Engineering Manager)
Week 3-4:    Solution research (L&D conducts market scan)
Week 5-6:    Vendor shortlist (3-5 solutions evaluated)
Week 7-8:    Pilot proposal (internal business case)
Week 9-12:   Pilot approval & procurement
Week 13-24:  Pilot execution (3 months)
Week 25-28:  Evaluation & expansion decision
```

**Average Sales Cycle: 4-6 months (first contract)**
**Expansion Cycle: 1-2 months (existing customer)**

## 4. Sales Approach

### 4.1 Direct Outreach vs Partnerships

**Phase 1 (Months 1-6): Direct Outreach ONLY**
- Why: Learn what works, iterate pitch, control narrative
- Focus: 50 target companies, personalized outreach
- Channels: LinkedIn, warm intros, industry events

**Phase 2 (Months 6-12): Selective Partnerships**
- Why: Scale what we've proven
- Partners: HR consultancies, training providers, enterprise software vendors
- Revenue share: 20-30% for partner-led deals

## 5. Competitive Differentiation

### 5.1 Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Type | Strength | Weakness | Our Advantage |
|------------|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| **Duolingo** | Consumer app | Brand recognition, free | Not workplace focused | Professional context |
| **EF Corporate** | Traditional training | Relationships, established | Expensive, not scalable | AI-powered, always available |
| **Cambly** | 1:1 tutoring | Human interaction | Expensive, scheduling friction | On-demand, consistent |
| **GoFluent** | B2B language | Enterprise features | Generic content | Culture-specific scenarios |
| **Internal Training** | In-house | Custom content | High cost, limited scale | Scalable, data-driven |

### 5.2 Unique Positioning

**Positioning Statement:**
> "Culturalbility is the only AI-powered training platform that prepares Chinese tech professionals for real workplace conversations with international colleagues — focusing on the specific accents, contexts, and cultural nuances that cause daily friction."

### 5.3 Key Differentiators

| Differentiator | Why It Matters | Proof Point |
|----------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Accent-specific training** | Indian, American, British accents are the real challenge | Demo shows understanding of different accents |
| **Workplace scenarios only** | Generic language training doesn't help in meetings | Scenario library: standups, code reviews, escalations |
| **AI-powered scalability** | 1:1 human training is too expensive | ¥1,500/year vs ¥50,000/year for tutors |
| **Cultural nuance** | It's not just language, it's communication style | Content reviewed by cultural consultants |
| **B2B-first analytics** | Managers need to see ROI | Dashboard shows usage, improvement, confidence |

### 5.4 Defensibility

**Short-term (1 year):**
- Proprietary scenario content
- Customer relationships and pilot success data
- First-mover in "Chinese tech worker" niche

**Medium-term (2-3 years):**
- Data flywheel: more users → better AI → better outcomes
- Brand recognition in target segment
- Integration partnerships (Slack, Teams, Zoom)

**Long-term (3+ years):**
- Expand to other emerging market professionals (Southeast Asia, Latin America)
- Become the standard for cross-cultural workplace communication
